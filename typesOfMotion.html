Motion

We can define motion as the change of position of an object with respect to time. A book falling off a table, water flowing from the tap, rattling windows are some examples of motion. Even the air that we breathe exhibits motion!

Types Of Motion

RECTILINEAR MOTION

The motion of the object in a straight-line path is called rectilinear motion. For example- motion of train on a straight track, the motion of ants in a straight path, the motion of freely falling stone from the top of the building towards the ground

CIRCULAR MOTION

The motion of an object in a circular path is called circular motion. For example- Car moving in a circular path, the movement of a tire of a car.

ROTATIONAL MOTION

It can also be defined as the motion of a body, in which all of its particles move in a circular motion with a common angular velocity, about a fixed point—for example, the rotation of Earth about its axis.

PERIODIC MOTION

Motion of an object which repeats itself after a certain period of time is called periodic motion. For example- Motion of Earth around the sun is periodic as well as circular as it repeats it motion after time of 365 days. Motion of simple pendulum is periodic.

OCILLATORY MOTION

Oscillatory motion can be termed as the repeated motion in which an object repeats the same movement over and over. In the absence of friction, the oscillatory motion would continue forever; but in the real world, the system eventually settles into equilibrium.

VIBRATORY MOTION

A type of motion in which a particle moves to and fro about a fixed point is known as vibratory motion. For example- strings of guitar, rubber membrane of table.
